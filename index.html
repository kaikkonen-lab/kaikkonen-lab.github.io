# Cardiovascular Epigenomics and Gene Regulation The Kaikkonen Lab investigates how genetic and epigenomic regulatory mechanisms shape atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease and cardiometabolic risk. We combine cellular and genetic model systems, single-cell transcriptomics, epigenomics, high-throughput functional genomics, and computational biology to uncover disease-relevant regulatory mechanisms. Our work focuses especially on the functional interpretation of non-coding GWAS variants. Using massively parallel reporter assays, CRISPR/Cas9 perturbation, cellular models, and multi-omics analysis, we aim to connect disease-associated genetic variation to regulatory mechanisms, cell states, and biological function.
