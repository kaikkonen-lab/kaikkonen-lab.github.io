Research mission

Cardiovascular disease is shaped by regulatory mechanisms that operate across genetic, epigenomic, transcriptional, and cellular layers.

Our lab combines functional genomics, disease models, and computational analysis to identify the regulatory programs that drive disease-relevant cell states and vascular inflammation.

Scientific focus

Three connected questions guide our work.

01

How does genetic variation influence regulatory function?

We investigate how disease-associated non-coding variants affect chromatin accessibility, enhancer activity, transcription factor binding, and gene expression.

02

Which cell states drive cardiovascular disease progression?

We study inflammatory, vascular, and metabolic cell-state programs involved in atherosclerosis and cardiometabolic disease.

03

How can multi-omics data reveal disease mechanisms?

We integrate genomics, epigenomics, transcriptomics, and computational models to prioritize mechanisms and candidate therapeutic targets.
