  <section class="mission-section">
    <div class="mission-label">Research mission</div>

    <div class="mission-text">
      <p>
        Cardiovascular disease is shaped by regulatory mechanisms that operate
        across genetic, epigenomic, transcriptional, and cellular layers.
      </p>

      <p>
        Our lab combines functional genomics, disease models, and computational
        analysis to identify the regulatory programs that drive disease-relevant
        cell states and vascular inflammation.
      </p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="research-pillars">
    <div class="section-heading">
      <p>Scientific focus</p>
      <h2>Three connected questions guide our work.</h2>
    </div>

    <div class="pillar-grid">
      <article>
        <span>01</span>
        <h3>How does genetic variation influence regulatory function?</h3>
        <p>
          We investigate how disease-associated non-coding variants affect
          chromatin accessibility, enhancer activity, transcription factor
          binding, and gene expression.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article>
        <span>02</span>
        <h3>Which cell states drive cardiovascular disease progression?</h3>
        <p>
          We study inflammatory, vascular, and metabolic cell-state programs
          involved in atherosclerosis and cardiometabolic disease.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article>
        <span>03</span>
        <h3>How can multi-omics data reveal disease mechanisms?</h3>
        <p>
          We integrate genomics, epigenomics, transcriptomics, and computational
          models to prioritize mechanisms and candidate therapeutic targets.
        </p>
      </article>
    </div>
  </section>
