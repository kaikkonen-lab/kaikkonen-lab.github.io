Research · Kaikkonen Lab

Regulatory genomics of cardiovascular disease

The Kaikkonen Lab studies how genetic variation, chromatin regulation, enhancer activity, cellular state transitions, and inflammatory programs contribute to atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease and cardiometabolic disease.

Recent publications Google Scholar PubMed

Research themes

Mechanisms linking genetic variation to disease biology

Our research connects statistical genetic discoveries with functional regulatory mechanisms, disease-associated cell states, and experimentally testable cardiovascular biology.

01

Regulatory variant function

We investigate how non-coding disease-associated variants influence enhancers, transcription factor binding, chromatin accessibility, and target gene regulation.

02

Single-cell and spatial disease biology

We use single-cell, multiome, and spatial approaches to identify disease-relevant cell states and regulatory programs in cardiovascular tissues.

03

Functional genomics and perturbation

We combine high-throughput functional genomics, cellular models, and computational analysis to prioritize mechanisms and interpret disease risk.

Research direction

From association to mechanism

Genetic discovery

Identify disease-associated loci and prioritize variants most likely to influence cardiovascular disease biology.

Regulatory mechanism

Connect variants to enhancers, chromatin programs, target genes, and disease-relevant cellular responses.

Biological interpretation

Translate genomic discoveries into mechanistic insight and experimentally testable hypotheses for cardiovascular disease.
